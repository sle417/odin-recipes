A Basic Lasagna Recipe

This lasagna recipe is a classic Italian dish that is hearty, cheesy, saucy, and immensely comforting. It features layers of pasta, homemade meat sauce, creamy ricotta, nutty Parmesan, and gooey mozzarella cheese. The recipe is easy to follow and includes tips, tricks, and freezer instructions. It's perfect for a cozy family dinner or a gathering with friends. The recipe can be customized to your liking, and you can add your own twist to it. Serve it with garlic bread and a salad, and you have a complete meal that everyone will love.

Ingredients:

  • 1 ½ pounds ground Italian sausage (or a combination of sausage and ground beef)
  • 1 medium white or yellow onion, diced
  • 6 large garlic cloves, minced
  • 3 tablespoons tomato paste
  • ½ teaspoon crushed red pepper flakes
  • 1 (28-ounce) can crushed tomatoes
  • 1 (15-ounce) can diced tomatoes
  • 2 teaspoons dried basil
  • 2 teaspoons dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • ½ teaspoon black pepper
  • 15 no-boil lasagna noodles
  • 3 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1 ½ cups grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 cups ricotta cheese
  • Fresh basil leaves for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a large skillet, cook the ground Italian sausage (or sausage and ground beef) over medium heat until browned. Drain any excess fat.
  2. Add the diced onion and minced garlic to the skillet and cook until the onion is translucent.
  3. Stir in the tomato paste and crushed red pepper flakes, and cook for 1-2 minutes.
  4. Add the crushed tomatoes, diced tomatoes, dried basil, dried oregano, salt, and black pepper. Simmer the sauce for 20-30 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  5. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  6. In a separate bowl, combine the ricotta cheese, 1 cup of grated Parmesan cheese, and a pinch of salt and pepper.
  7. Spread a thin layer of the meat sauce on the bottom of a lightly greased casserole dish.
  8. Place a layer of lasagna noodles on top of the sauce, followed by a third of the ricotta cheese mixture, a third of the meat sauce, and a third of the shredded mozzarella cheese. Repeat this layering process two more times.
  9. Sprinkle the remaining ½ cup of grated Parmesan cheese on top.
  10. Cover the dish with foil and bake for 30 minutes. Then, remove the foil and bake for an additional 15 minutes until the cheese is melted and bubbly.
  11. Let the lasagna rest for 10-15 minutes before serving. Garnish with fresh basil leaves if desired.
